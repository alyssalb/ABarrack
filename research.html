Scholarship

Research & Writing

My scholarly work spans dramaturgy, horror genre studies, digital humanities, AI and performance, and queer media criticism. I'm committed to writing that is rigorous and opinionated — and to forms, both traditional and digital, that make arguments accessible.

Samples

Writing Samples

A selection of published and produced work in different registers — scholarly, digital, and dramaturgical. Each represents a different way I think through and with form.

Digital EssayOpen Access

Cozy Horror: A Digital Essay

A hybrid scholarly work combining textual analysis with Python-generated color barcodes and network visualizations to identify the shared characteristics of an emerging horror subgenre. Demonstrates computational methods alongside traditional media scholarship.

ZinePublished 2026

The Drolls: A Dramaturgical Mini-Zine Program

Published in Academizines: A Special Issue of Unbound: A Journal of Digital Scholarship. A zine-format dramaturgical program that uses the form itself to argue for audience-centered dramaturgy practice. Eds. Dr. Spencer Keralis and Professor Zach Frazier.

Digital WorkOpen Access

Passing Notes: An Experiment in Digital Care

A generative digital artwork — also a making-statement about who gets to code and why. The project itself is the argument: DIY queer feminist digital making as a critical practice, inspired by Design Justice and Your Computer Is On Fire.

Hitting a Little Too Close to Home

Reflexivity, Liminality, and Identification in Horror across Film, Theatre, and Games. MA Thesis, UCF, 2022. An interdisciplinary examination of horror's relationship with audience identification and reflexivity — the scholarly roots of ongoing work in horror genre studies.
